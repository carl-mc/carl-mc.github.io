About Me

I am a PostDoc at the International Conflict Research Group at ETH Zurich and a visiting researcher at Harvard University’s Institute for Quantitative Social Science. My recently finished dissertation focuses on state building in twentieth century Africa and its effects on local development and conflict. At ETH Zurich, I am part of the ERC NASTAC project that analyzes the interplay between nationalism and state (trans)formation in Europe. Furthermore, I work on the effects of local ethnic demography on pre-election violence and ethnic favoritism in Africa.

To carry out my research, I have collected various sets of mostly geospatial data and have developed new measures of state reach, colonial indirect rule, and ethnic geographies.
