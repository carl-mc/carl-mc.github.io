About Me

I am a Departmental Lecturer at University of Oxford’s Department of Politics and International Relations and a Tutor at St. Hilda’s College. Currently, I am visiting Harvard University’s Institute for Quantitative Social Science. Previously, I was a PostDoc at the International Conflict Research Group at ETH Zurich. My research focuses on state building and its effects on local development and conflict, with a focus on twentieth century Africa. Furthermore, I work on ethnic politics in Africa, in particular analyzing patterns of ethnic electoral violence and favoritism.

To carry out my research, I use innovative computational methods to collect large, mostly geospatial sets and derive new measures of state reach, colonial indirect rule, and ethnic geographies. My research is published or accepted at International Organization, the British Journal of Political Science, and the Journal of Peace Research.
